Recording Tips & Resources

Lighting and camera setup advice

Create professional-looking video

Position your main light source in front of you, not behind. Natural light from a window works well, or use a ring light for even illumination. Place your camera at eye level and ensure your face is well-lit without harsh shadows.

Audio Quality

Clear audio is crucial for effective communication

Record in a quiet room with minimal echo. Use a dedicated microphone if possible, even a basic lavalier mic will significantly improve audio quality compared to built-in laptop mics. Test your audio before recording your final take.

Presentation Tips

How to appear confident and professional

Dress professionally as you would for an actual interview. Maintain good posture and eye contact with the camera. Speak clearly and at a moderate pace. Practice your responses but avoid sounding rehearsed. Use hand gestures naturally but avoid excessive movement.
